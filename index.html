Real consulting engagement with a Ghanaian property firm — analysed booking patterns across two short-stay properties, identified overpricing as the root cause of low occupancy, and delivered a data-driven repricing strategy that generated a +290% revenue uplift. Built end-to-end: Python EDA → Excel workbook → Power BI & Tableau dashboards → live interactive Plotly Dash app deployed on Render.

PythonPandasPlotly DashPower BITableauEDAExcelRender
Live App → | Tableau → | Power BI → | GitHub →
ProblemTwo short-stay properties launched at prices too high for the Kumasi market — Barekese at 18% occupancy, Buokrom at 15%. The client had no data infrastructure to diagnose why bookings were low or what the right price point should be.
ApproachStructured three years of raw booking records into clean datasets and ran full EDA in Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) to identify occupancy trends by pricing phase. Benchmarked against the local Kumasi market, built a phase-by-phase repricing model, and delivered findings through Power BI for operations management and Tableau for executive and investor reporting.
OutcomeBarekese occupancy rose from 18% → 58% after repricing, generating a +290% revenue uplift. Buokrom rose from 15% → 61% (+196%). All four portfolio locations across Ashanti and Greater Accra analysed and delivered in a live Plotly Dash app and structured Excel workbook.
